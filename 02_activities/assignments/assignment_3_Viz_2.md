For each visualization, describe and justify:

What software did you use to create your data visualization?

I used Microsoft Excel to create the data visualization, specifically a horizontal bar chart representing grant allocations by program area. Excel was selected for its accessibility, familiarity to a broad user base, and ability to quickly create and edit charts from tabular data.

Who is your intended audience?

The visualization targets city residents, policymakers, and community organizations interested in how Toronto’s community grants were distributed across sectors. 

What information or message are you trying to convey with your visualization?

The visualization aims to highlight how Toronto allocated funding across various community programs in 2024. It emphasizes which areas received the most financial support, allowing viewers to compare investment priorities across initiatives.

What aspects of design did you consider when making your visualization? How did you apply them? With what elements of your plots?

Clarity and readability: Horizontal bars allow longer program names to be shown.

Labeling: Each bar is labeled directly to avoid forcing users to refer to a legend.

Ordering: Bars are sorted in descending order to show highest-to-lowest allocations, improving comparative scanning.

How did you ensure that your data visualizations are reproducible? If the tool you used to make your data visualization is not reproducible, how will this impact your data visualization?

All steps—from selecting data to generating the chart—were done within the Excel file, stored on a clearly labeled "Graphs" tab. The source data is located in the same workbook under separate tabs. Anyone with access to the file can easily recreate or modify the chart. 

How did you ensure that your data visualization is accessible?

Plain fonts and high-contrast colors were used.

Axis labels and bar labels were added to minimize cognitive load.

Who are the individuals and communities who might be impacted by your visualization?

This visualization impacts local community organizations, especially those relying on public grants. City officials and advocates may also use it to justify resource shifts. 

How did you choose which features of your chosen dataset to include or exclude from your visualization?

The original dataset contains organization names, funding amounts, and program categories. For this visualization, I aggregated the data by program category, excluding organization-level detail to emphasize broader funding patterns. This highlights sector allocation rather recepients.


What ‘underwater labour’ contributed to your final data visualization product?

Testing multiple chart types before selecting the horizontal bar for its clarity. Formatting elements (axes, gridlines, font sizes) for readability.
